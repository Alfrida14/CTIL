Correct Answer:- Only police officers authorized for traffic duty

Only traffic police officers or other officials specifically authorized by the state government can issue challans. While RTO officers can check vehicles for registration, tax, or permit violations, they do not issue instant on-road fines in most cases. CCTV cameras can record violations, but only authorized officers can approve and issue the challan. This ensures that penalties are legally valid and properly recorded.
